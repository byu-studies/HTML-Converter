Gathering

Ah,

Child

Merry,

Wreathe

Bridal wreath

In June,

Lace daisy strands

Through summer,

Chain dandelions

Stem on stem,

And wear them

Through October.

Blossom read

The yellow chins

Of those who love

Sweet butter,

Pluck petals

Of forget-me-nots,

Enclose them in a letter.

Snap dragons

To your fingers five,

And sip on honey suckle.

Kiss the friendly columbine,

Twine up the purple myrtle.

Wear rings of woven clover

Sweet,

For earrings dangle cherries,

Dine with dolls of hollyhock

On willow sap and berries.

O golden ribbons

From the sun,

O silver ribbons

Of the moon,

Go, my child

And gather, gather,

That which passes

All too soon.

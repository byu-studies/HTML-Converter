January Night

Once the snow has fallen,

moonlight becomes 

superfluous.

Winterlight,

shadow-friend,

suffuses all.

The difference between night

and day

is a degree of iridescence.

No creature slinks towards us

from the umbral woods,

fangs dripping.

We are the creatures.

We are the woods.

We are the light 

and the shadow,

all slumbering together

in the glow-dark hush.

—Susan Jeffers

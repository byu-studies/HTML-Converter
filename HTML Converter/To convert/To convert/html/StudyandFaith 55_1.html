The Book of Mormon Critical Text Project

We are pleased to announce three sets of books by Royal Skousen that are now available for purchase (use attached order form).

Grammatical Variation, parts 1 and 2 of volume 3, The History of the Text of the Book of Mormon, by Royal Skousen

2 volumes, 1,304 total pages, hardcover, $108.74 (price includes shipping)

Newly published, this two-volume set analyzes every basic type of editorial change or grammatical variation in the Book of Mormon, beginning with the handwritten manuscripts and considering every major printed edition.

Analysis of Textual Variants of the Book of Mormon, by Royal Skousen

6 volumes, 4,060 total pages, hardcover, $165.74 (price includes shipping)

These books consider every significant textual change that has occurred in the English Book of Mormon over the 187 years since Joseph Smith first dictated it to his scribes; it also considers a number of conjectural emendations for specific words or passages.

The Original Manuscript and the Printer’s Manuscript of the Book of Mormon, by Royal Skousen

3 volumes, 1,535 total pages, hardcover, $58.74 (price includes shipping)

A stray ink drop and a quirk of nineteenth-century script make the difference between “retain that wrong” and “repair that wrong.” More than a decade of meticulous research revealed such insights as Royal Skousen prepared transcripts of the original and printer’s manuscripts of the Book of Mormon for publication.

George Handley Joins Advisory Board

We are pleased to announce that George Handley has joined the BYU Studies Advisory Board. Handley is an associate dean in the BYU College of Humanities and former chair of the Department of Humanities, Classics, and Comparative Literature. He is the author of several books, as well as “A Poetics of the Restoration,” published in BYU Studies 49, no. 4. Handley previously served on the BYU Studies Liberal Arts and Sciences Editorial Board.

Remembering Stephen Webb

Our thoughts and prayers are with the family of Dr. Stephen Webb at this time of his passing. Webb was a Catholic theologian who took an enthusiastic interest in Mormonism. BYU Studies Quarterly published a review essay by Webb on systematizing Mormon theology, a chapter from his book Jesus Christ, Eternal God, and a review by Mauro Properzi of Webb’s book on Mormon theology, Mormon Christianity: What Other Christians Can Learn from the Latter-day Saints.

Spring Discounts on Selected BYU Studies Books

BYU Studies regularly discounts selected titles as a service to our subscribers. The prices for the four books featured below are available only by using the accompanying order form. For more information about these books, go to byustudies.byu.edu/books.aspx.

An Advocate for Women: The Public Life of Emmeline B. Wells, 1870–1920, by Carol Cornwall Madsen

512 pages, hardcover, $29.95 $15.00

In her fifty years as a public figure, Emmeline B. Wells edited the Woman’s Exponent, represented Latter-day Saint women in national women’s organizations, courageously defended her religion in the halls of Congress, and helped mitigate anti-Mormon sentiments, all before becoming Relief Society General President in 1910 at age eighty-two.

Mountain Meadows Massacre: The Andrew Jenson and David H. Morris Collections, edited by Richard E. Turley Jr. and Ronald W. Walker

352 pages, hardcover, $44.95 $20.00

During research for their 2008 book Massacre at Mountain Meadows: An American Tragedy, Ronald W. Walker, Richard E. Turley Jr., Glen M. Leonard, and their colleagues uncovered a great deal of new information about the 1857 massacre, including two major collections of documents from people who participated in the massacre or knew others who did. The first was gathered in the 1890s by Andrew Jenson (1850–1937), a full-time employee in the Church Historian’s Office, and the second a decade or two later by David H. Morris (1858–1937), an attorney and judge in St. George, Utah.

This volume presents full-color images of original documents accompanied by typed transcriptions. Brief biographical sketches introduce the individuals who provided the information.

No Toil nor Labor Fear: The Story of William Clayton, by James B. Allen

454 pages, softcover, $15.99 $6.00

As Joseph Smith’s friend and scribe, Clayton kept extensive journals and even wrote the first history of the Nauvoo Temple. As a pioneer, he penned the words to the hymn “Come, Come, Ye Saints,” and compiled the Latter-day Saints’ Emigrants’ Guide. His life encompassed nearly all the joys and struggles that could come to a Church member of his day, yet “no toil nor labor” did he fear.

T. Edgar Lyon: A Teacher in Zion, by Thomas Edgar Lyon Jr.

360 pages, softcover $18.95 $3.00

A teacher at the Salt Lake Institute of Religion for three decades, T. Edgar Lyon regularly drew more students than could squeeze into his classroom. Lyon’s gift as a vivid storyteller made Church history come alive. Dr. Lyon, eyes twinkling, would ask: “Why did Brigham Young choose oxen over horses or mules to move wagons westward?” “Better gas mileage,” Lyon beamed. “They could survive on poor grass without supplemental grain, and they ate less in comparison to the weight they pulled.”

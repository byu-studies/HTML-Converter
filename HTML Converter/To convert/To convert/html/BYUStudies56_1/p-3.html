The Kingdom of God

(After James Goldberg)

is not the soil. It’s the weed

parting loam between lots,

snaking through sod not yet

rooted to earth.

The Kingdom of God

is not the seed. It’s the husk

gone to ground when the seedling

sheds its vestments and stretches

toward the sun.

The Kingdom of God

is not the tree. It’s the shrub

reaching ragged branches

through the neighbor’s fence,

imposing sprouts in your lawn.

The Kingdom of God

is not the plant. It’s the dodder

entangling the plant. It’s

the morning glory’s herald,

the mistletoe’s kiss, the broomrape’s tongue

persuading the earth to give way.

—Tyler Chadwick

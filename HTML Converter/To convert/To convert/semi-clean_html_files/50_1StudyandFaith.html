New Book from BYU Studies

A Love of Learning: The Speeches of Robert K. Thomas,
edited by Daryl R. Hague

246 pages, softcover, $14.95

The name Robert K. Thomas will be familiar to many long-time BYU Studies subscribers. Thomas (1918–1998) served the BYU community for thirty-two years as an English professor, founder and director of the Honors Program, and academic vice president. He received the distinction of becoming the first BYU Presidential Fellow. In 1961, students selected him as Professor of the Year, and in 1966 he received the Karl G. Maeser Award for Teaching Excellence.

Through these speeches, Thomas offers his insight into many facets of wisdom and Christian discipleship. He inspires scholars, students, and all people seeking a self-examined life to find joy and fulfillment through study and faith.

New Online Subscription

BYU Studies is pleased to announce a new online subscription. An online subscription costs $24 per year, compared to $30 for a regular subscription. All regular and online subscribers can access the current journal from any online source and also have complete access to recent issues that are unavailable to nonsubscribers. Online subscribers and all regular subscribers for whom we have email addresses will receive an email alerting them to the latest journal release as it becomes available. Only regular subscribers receive the printed journal through the mail.

Charting the New Testament

Now available on our website to all subscribers: over 200 New Testament charts. These come from the book Charting the New Testament by John W. Welch and John F. Hall, published by FARMS in 2002. All subscribers may now use these charts in their own gospel study. You can access these charts by first logging in with your username and password and then selecting the following links on our menu bar: Study Resources > New Testament > New Testament Charts. If you are a subscriber and have not yet logged in on our website, please provide us with your email address and we will help you set up your Web account so that you can access all our premium content.

BYU Studies Announces Editorial Fellowships

BYU Studies has created a volunteer position for new PhDs and upper-level graduate students who want to be involved in Mormon studies. These editorial fellows will be listed on the BYU Studies masthead, will gain access to the network of Mormon studies scholars, and will get experience in academic publishing. Fellows will be expected to assist BYU Studies in reviewing article submissions and suggesting books to be reviewed. To apply to become an editorial fellow, please contact byustudies@byu.edu. We have recently welcomed these four editorial fellows: Ronan Head, Brent Schmidt, Ryan Tobler, and Lynne Wilson. Dr. Schmidt has an article in this issue of BYU Studies.

Scholars Supporting BYU Studies

The BYU Studies Academy, a group of invited scholars supporting BYU Studies behind the scenes, met February 26, 2011, at BYU. We heard from Clark Gilbert, president and CEO of Deseret News and Deseret Digital Media, who discussed ways the Church is using the Web to promote its message in its own voice. He suggested possibilities for making BYU Studies more prominent on the Web, and we look forward to implementing his suggestions. We encourage all readers to visit our website and find us on Facebook or Twitter.

Spring Book Bargains

Art and Spirituality: The Visual Culture of Christian Faith, edited by Herman du Toit and Doris R. Dant. 320 pages, softcover $21.95 $12.00. This collection of seventeen articles discusses a variety of topics on the interaction between religion and art. Each article is an expansion of a presentation given at the BYU “Art, Belief, Meaning: Pious Pictures” symposium in 2006. Art enthusiasts will especially enjoy this full-color book with 106 illustrations.

Discoveries: Two Centuries of Mormon Poems, 2d ed., compiled and edited by Susan Elizabeth Howe and Sheree Maxwell Bench. 122 pages, hardcover $14.95 $10.00. This beautiful gift book presents fifty-two poems that trace Mormon women’s life experiences. The poetry stirs readers to remember, to ponder, often to laugh, sometimes to weep, yet always to rejoice.

Turning Freud Upside Down: Gospel Perspectives on Psychotherapy’s Fundamental Problems, edited by Aaron P. Jackson and Lane Fischer, with Doris R. Dant. 256 pages, softcover $16.95 $8.00. “If you want to think about psychotherapy in dramatically new ways, this book upends traditional psychological dogma. Far more important, it also advances alternative, gospel-based views of human behavior and personality. LDS and other Christian clinicians who feel lost in the trenches will find this book an indispensable map for moving further away from secular assumptions and techniques to a more spiritual base.” —Godfrey J. Ellis, PhD, Director of the Master’s Program in Counseling Psychology, St. Martin’s University

The Worlds of Joseph Smith: A Bicentennial Conference at the Library of Congress, edited by John W. Welch. 325 pages, softcover $24.95 $15.00. These papers, presented at the international academic conference held at the Library of Congress in Washington, D.C., in recognition of the bicentennial of Joseph Smith’s birth, position Joseph’s life and mission within the larger framework of American spirituality and world religions.
